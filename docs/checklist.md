1. Power Up & Initial Setup
Captain:

Batteries: Turn ON BAT 1 and BAT 2 (Overhead panel).

External Power: Turn ON EXT PWR (if the green "AVAIL" light is showing).

First Officer:

ADIRS (Inertial Reference): Set knobs 1, 2, and 3 to NAV. (Wait for the "ON BAT" light to extinguish between each one).

Crew Oxygen: Press to turn ON.

Exterior Lights: Turn NAV & LOGO lights ON, set Strobe to AUTO.

Fire Tests: Perform system tests for the APU and both Engines (hold the test buttons and verify alarms/lights).

2. Flight Computer & Pre-Flight
Captain:

Glareshield (FCU): Turn on the Flight Director (FD) and set the local altimeter pressure (QNH).

Instruments: Verify the Primary Flight Display (PFD) and Navigation Display (ND) are aligning and showing no unexpected errors.

Parking Brake: Verify the handle is ON and brake pressure is indicated.

MCDU (Flight Computer) Setup: Follow the standard "DIFSRIP" flow:

Data: Check aircraft database validity.

INIT A: Enter From/To airports, Flight Number, Cost Index, and Cruise Altitude.

F-PLN: Enter the departure (SID), routing, and arrival (STAR).

RAD NAV: Verify navigation radios are auto-tuned.

INIT B: Enter Zero Fuel Weight (ZFW) and Block Fuel.

PERF: Enter V1, VR, V2 speeds, Transition Altitude, and Takeoff Flap setting.

First Officer:

Fuel Pumps: Turn all 6 wing and center tank pumps ON.

Signs: Set Seatbelt signs to AUTO, No Smoking to AUTO, and Emergency Exit Lights to ARM.

3. APU Start & Pushback
Captain:

APU Start: Press APU MASTER SW ON, wait 3 seconds, then press APU START.

APU Bleed: Once the APU start button shows a green "AVAIL", turn ON APU BLEED (this provides air conditioning and engine starting pressure).

External Power: Disconnect EXT PWR.

Beacon Light: Turn ON (this tells the ground crew the engines are about to start).

First Officer:

Clearance: Request IFR Clearance from ATC then pushback and engine start clearance from ATC.

Transponder: Set to AUTO or ON.

Doors: Verify all cabin and cargo doors are closed on the ECAM door page.

4. Engine Start Sequence 

Captain:

Engine Mode Selector: Switch from NORM to IGN/START.

Engine 2: Switch Engine 2 Master ON. Monitor N1 and EGT gauges on the center display as the engine spools up.

Engine 1: Once Engine 2 is fully stabilized (when "AVAIL" appears on the engine display), switch Engine 1 Master ON.

Engine Mode Selector: Return the selector to NORM after both engines are stabilized.

First Officer:

Monitoring: Watch the engine parameters alongside the Captain and call out "Starter Valve Open", "Fuel Flow", and "Stabilized" for each engine.

5. After Start & Taxi
Captain:

Taxi: Release the Parking Brake, apply slight thrust to break away, and use the steering tiller to taxi to the runway.

First Officer:

APU Shutdown: Turn APU Bleed OFF, then APU Master SW OFF.

Ground Spoilers: Pull the speedbrake lever up slightly to ARM the ground spoilers.

Flaps: Set the flaps to the takeoff position calculated in the MCDU (usually Config 1 or 2).

Auto-Brake: Press to set Auto-Brakes to MAX for rejected takeoff protection.

ECAM: Check for any remaining memos or system warnings.

6. Before Takeoff (Approaching Runway)
Captain:

Runway Entry: Turn ON Nose Takeoff lights and Runway Turnoff lights when cleared to line up.

Takeoff Briefing: Confirm "Takeoff briefing complete".

First Officer:

Cabin: Use the chime or PA to call "Cabin crew, prepare for takeoff."

TCAS (Traffic): Set the transponder to TA/RA.

Engine Anti-Ice: Turn ON if conditions are cold and visible moisture is present.

Exterior Lights: Switch Strobes to ON, and turn Landing Lights ON once cleared for takeoff.

7. Takeoff & Climb
Captain:

Takeoff Roll: Advance thrust levers smoothly to 50% N1, let engines stabilize, then advance to FLX/MCT (or TOGA). Maintain runway centerline using rudder pedals.

Rotation: At the "Rotate" callout, gently pull back on the sidestick to establish the climb attitude.

Landing Gear: Command "Gear Up" once a positive rate of climb is established.

Autopilot: Engage Autopilot 1 (AP1) once safely airborne and stable (typically above 400 feet AGL).

Thrust Reduction: At the Thrust Reduction Altitude (flashing "LVR CLB" on the PFD), pull the thrust levers back into the CL (Climb) detent. Auto-thrust is now active.

First Officer:

Callouts: Call out "Thrust Set", "100 Knots", "V1", and "Rotate" at the respective speeds calculated in the MCDU.

Landing Gear: Move the Landing Gear lever UP upon the Captain's command.

Exterior Lights: Turn OFF Runway Turnoff and Nose Takeoff lights once the gear is up.

Flaps: Retract flaps on schedule as the aircraft accelerates, based on the PFD speed tape ("F" and "S" speeds).

Altimeter: Passing the Transition Altitude, push the altimeter knob to set it to STD (Standard) and cross-check with the Captain.

8. Cruise
First Officer:

Lighting: Turn OFF Seatbelt signs when above the weather and cruising smoothly. Turn them back ON if turbulence is expected.

9. Descent Preparation
Captain:

Flight Computer: Enter and verify the arrival, approach, STAR and transition.

PERF APPR: Enter destination QNH, temperature, wind and minima.

Descent Review: Review the descent profile, constraints and landing configuration data.

10. Approach & Landing
Captain:

Descent Management: Manage the aircraft's speed and altitude using the FCU (Glareshield) to comply with ATC instructions and the approach profile.

Configuration: Command flap extensions ("Flaps 1", "Flaps 2", etc.) as the speed bleeds off.

Landing Gear: Command "Gear Down" around 2000 feet AGL or when intercepting the glideslope.

Landing: Disconnect the Autopilot (usually by 500 feet AGL) for a manual landing, or monitor the systems for a full autoland.

Touchdown: At 20 feet, retard thrust levers to IDLE, flare the aircraft, and once on the ground, deploy reverse thrust.

First Officer:

Auto-Brake: Select LOW for landing.

Altimeter: Passing the Transition Level, pull the altimeter knob and set the destination local QNH.

Configuration: Extend flaps and drop the landing gear upon the Captain's command.

Ground Spoilers: Pull the speedbrake lever up slightly to ARM the ground spoilers.

Signs & Lights: Turn Seatbelt signs ON. Turn ON Landing Lights and Nose Taxi light.

Callouts: Call out "Approaching Minimums" and "Minimums" during the final descent. After touchdown, verify and call "Spoilers, Reverse Green, Decel".

11. After Landing & Taxi
Captain:

Deceleration: Disengage reverse thrust at 70 knots. Take over manual braking before taxi speed is reached.

Runway Exit: Vacate the runway smoothly.

First Officer:

Automatic Trigger: Monitor reverse thrust stowed by 70 knots. At 30 knots or below, begin the after-landing flow without requesting an app confirmation.

Exterior Lights: Turn OFF Landing Lights and Strobe Lights. Set the Nose light to TAXI.

Ground Spoilers: Push the speedbrake lever down to disarm them automatically.

Flaps: Retract flaps fully to zero automatically.

APU: Press APU MASTER SW ON, then APU START ON.

Radar & TCAS: Keep the WXR/PWS system selector at position 1. Do not operate the unsupported Captain or F/O radar display selectors. Set the Transponder to STBY.

Engine Anti-Ice: Pending aircraft-specific command and independent readback. Do not block the automatic flow or guess a generic event.

12. Parking & Shutdown (Securing the Aircraft)
Captain:

Parking: Steer the aircraft into the gate using the visual docking system or marshaller. Stop the aircraft.

Parking Brake: Set Parking Brake ON. The First Officer monitors the aircraft stopping and the brake being set; no app confirmation is required.

Engine Shutdown: Once the APU "AVAIL" light is on (or external power is connected), switch Engine 1 and Engine 2 Master switches to OFF. The First Officer detects shutdown automatically.

First Officer:

Exterior Lights: Automatically turn OFF the Nose Taxi light and, after both engines are off, the Beacon light.

Fuel Pumps: Automatically turn OFF all 6 wing and center tank fuel pumps.

Signs: Automatically turn OFF Seatbelt signs.

APU Bleed: Automatically turn ON to provide air conditioning to the cabin.

Doors: Monitor until a required cabin or cargo door is opened. Slide disarming remains part of the crew/ground operation because no independent aircraft readback is available.

Captain & First Officer (Final Cold & Dark Secure)
(If leaving the aircraft completely unpowered)

Crew Oxygen: Turn OFF.

Emergency Lights: Turn OFF.

Exterior Lights: Turn OFF NAV & LOGO lights.

ADIRS: Turn all three IRS knobs to OFF.

APU & Electrical Power: Turn APU Bleed OFF. Disconnect external power if it is connected while the APU is still available, then turn APU Master OFF and wait for the APU exhaust/intake flap to fully close.

Batteries: Turn OFF BAT 1 and BAT 2. Cold-and-dark actions begin only after explicit confirmation in the app so a normal turnaround is not powered down accidentally.
